Bangalore, officially Bengaluru, is the capital of Karnataka, known as the Silicon Valley of India and famed for its gardens, history, and vibrant culture.
Historical Background
Bangalore has a rich history dating back to at least 4000 BCE, with archaeological evidence of early human settlements. The city’s name originates from the Kannada phrase bende kaalu ooru, meaning town of boiled beans, linked to a legend of King Veera Ballala II of the Hoysala dynasty. Modern Bangalore was founded in 1537 by Kempe Gowda I under the Vijayanagara Empire, who built a mud fort that became the nucleus of the city. Over centuries, it was ruled by the Marathas, Mughals, and British, shaping its architecture and culture
